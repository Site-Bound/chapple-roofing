2. Services

Credvanta provides commercial debt recovery services including:

  • Pre-legal collections (letters, calls, emails, SMS)
  • Letter Before Action (LBA)
  • Negotiation of repayment arrangements
  • Field visit (doorstep recovery) services
  • Trace & locate services
  • Referral to third-party legal representatives where required

Credvanta acts as a debt recovery agent only and is not a firm of solicitors, sheriff officers, certificated enforcement agents, or bailiffs.

Credvanta reserves the right to refuse, suspend, or cease recovery activity on any account at its sole discretion, including where:

  • The debt is disputed;
  • The Client provides inaccurate or misleading information;
  • Credvanta believes continued action may breach applicable law or regulation; or
  • Recovery is deemed commercially unviable.

As part of Credvanta’s operational structure and compliance procedures, all Clients are required to utilise Credvanta’s partnered payment processing and merchant facility arrangement in connection with recovery services provided by Credvanta. The purpose of this structure is to ensure that debtor payments are settled directly into the Client’s designated merchant account and that Credvanta does not hold, receive, safeguard, or control client money within its own business bank accounts.

The Client acknowledges and agrees that:

  • A merchant account and/or payment facility will be established in the Client’s business name through one of Credvanta’s partnered Payment Providers;
  • The merchant facility shall be used solely for the purpose of collecting payments relating to debts, invoices, repayment arrangements, settlements, or balances instructed to Credvanta for recovery on the Client’s behalf;
  • Debtor payments processed through Credvanta’s systems, payment links, repayment arrangements, hosted payment pages, payment workflows, or recovery communications shall be settled directly into the Client’s designated merchant or settlement account;
  • Credvanta acts solely as a commercial debt recovery and servicing agent and does not provide regulated payment services;
  • Payment processing services are provided exclusively by independent third-party regulated Payment Providers;
  • The Client will receive separate merchant facility information, terms, and onboarding documentation directly from the relevant Payment Provider where applicable;
  • Credvanta may facilitate payment collection workflows, automated payment requests, repayment arrangements, hosted payment pages, payment communications, and reconciliation processes on the Client’s behalf;
  • Credvanta will cover all payment processing and operational costs associated with the merchant facility;
  • Fixed payment processing charges currently consist of approximately 1% plus interchange rates and approximately £0.03 per transaction;
  • Credvanta will cover all payment processing and operational costs associated with the merchant facility as part of its service model. Such costs may be reflected internally within Credvanta’s invoicing, reconciliation, commission, or credit note processes but shall not create additional standalone merchant processing charges payable by the Client beyond the fees expressly agreed within these Terms & Conditions.
  • The Client agrees to maintain an active rolling monthly service arrangement with Credvanta while utilising Credvanta’s recovery and payment facilitation services;

  • The merchant facility and related services will be cancelled or closed upon termination of the relationship subject to the requirements of the relevant Payment Provider;
  • Termination shall not affect any accrued commissions, processing charges, reconciliation adjustments, minimum usage fees, or other outstanding sums due prior to termination.

Credvanta reserves the right to suspend, restrict, or terminate services where: a Payment Provider declines, suspends, restricts, or withdraws merchant facilities; fraud risks, excessive chargebacks, unlawful activity, or compliance concerns are identified; the Client breaches these Terms & Conditions; or continued servicing is considered commercially, operationally, or legally unsuitable.

4. Commission Entitlement

Credvanta shall be entitled to all applicable fees and commissions where:

  • Payment is recovered following Credvanta involvement;
  • Payment is made directly to the Client following Credvanta involvement;
  • A payment arrangement is agreed during the engagement period; or
  • Settlement is reached within 6 months of Credvanta’s last action on the account.

Commission shall be calculated on the total amount recovered, including instalment arrangements, negotiated settlements, partial settlements, and direct payments made to the Client. The Client must notify Credvanta within 3 business days of receiving any direct payment relating to an instructed account. Any attempt to deliberately avoid payment of Credvanta’s fees or commissions, including settlement of instructed Cases without notification to Credvanta, shall constitute a material breach of these Terms & Conditions.

Where Credvanta reasonably believes that payments have been received and not disclosed, Credvanta reserves the right to immediately invoice:

  • The full 15% commission calculated against the total original invoice value of the instructed Case(s);
  • The applicable £35 fixed recovery fee per instructed invoice(s); and
  • Reasonable administrative, audit, tracing, reconciliation, legal, and recovery costs incurred as a result of the breach;
  • Statutory interest and late payment compensation charges pursuant to the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

In circumstances where the Client’s failure to disclose payments materially prevents Credvanta from accurately determining recoveries across active instructed Cases, Credvanta reserves the right to estimate commissions due based on available account information, payment history, recovery performance, and other reasonably available evidence. The burden of demonstrating that no recovery has occurred shall rest with the Client where Credvanta has reasonable grounds to believe payment disclosure obligations have been breached.
