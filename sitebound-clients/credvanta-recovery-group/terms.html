Terms & Conditions

Applies to all clients instructing Credvanta Recovery Group

1. Definitions

  • "Credvanta" refers to Credvanta Recovery Group, being the trading name used by Keiran Harvey trading as a sole trader.

2. Services

Credvanta provides commercial debt recovery services including:

  • Pre-legal collections (letters, calls, emails, SMS)
  • Letter Before Action (LBA)
  • Negotiation of repayment arrangements
  • Field visit (doorstep recovery) services
  • Trace & locate services
  • Referral to third-party legal representatives where required

Credvanta acts as a debt recovery agent only and is not a firm of solicitors, sheriff officers, certificated enforcement agents, or bailiffs.

Credvanta reserves the right to refuse, suspend, or cease recovery activity on any account at its sole discretion, including where:

  • The debt is disputed;
  • The Client provides inaccurate or misleading information;
  • Credvanta believes continued action may breach applicable law or regulation; or
  • Recovery is deemed commercially unviable.

  • £75 – first visit

  • Field visit fees are payable where a visit has been conducted regardless of recovery outcome.

4. Commission Entitlement

Commission becomes due and payable to Credvanta upon the recovery of any funds from a Debtor, whether by way of a lump sum, partial payment, or payment arrangement.

Commission is calculated on all sums received from the Debtor in respect of the instructed Debt, including any statutory interest, compensation, or court costs recovered.

Where a Debtor makes payment directly to the Client following Credvanta's intervention (including where the Debtor contacts the Client directly to settle), commission shall remain due and payable in full as if Credvanta had collected the funds directly.

Where a Client withdraws an account after Credvanta has commenced recovery activity and the Debtor subsequently pays the Client within 12 months of withdrawal, commission shall remain due and payable.

Credvanta reserves the right to pursue commission entitlement through the courts where a Client fails to pay commission properly due.

5. Client Obligations

The Client agrees to:

  • Provide accurate and complete information in respect of each Debt submitted, including debtor details, invoice documentation, and any relevant correspondence.
  • Notify Credvanta immediately if a Debtor makes direct contact with the Client or makes any payment, however partial, in respect of an instructed Debt.
  • Notify Credvanta immediately of any dispute raised by a Debtor.
  • Not take any action in respect of an instructed Debt without first notifying Credvanta.
  • Respond promptly to requests for information or documentation from Credvanta.
  • Ensure that all debts submitted are genuine, commercially enforceable, and not subject to any set-off, counter-claim, or dispute that has not been disclosed.

6. Authority to Act

By instructing Credvanta, the Client authorises Credvanta to:

  • Contact the Debtor by any lawful means, including in writing, by telephone, by email, by SMS, and in person.
  • Negotiate payment arrangements on the Client's behalf.
  • Issue a Letter Before Action (LBA) in the Client's name or in Credvanta's name as recovery agent.
  • Conduct or arrange field visits to the Debtor's known address(es).
  • Instruct third-party trace agents, credit reference agencies, or legal representatives as required.
  • Process Debtor personal data as a data processor on the Client's behalf for the purposes of recovery activity.

7. Disputed Accounts

Where a Debtor raises a dispute regarding a submitted Debt, Credvanta will notify the Client promptly and may suspend recovery activity pending the Client's response.

Credvanta does not adjudicate on disputes between Clients and Debtors. It is the Client's responsibility to resolve any dispute and to provide Credvanta with clear instructions on how to proceed.

Credvanta reserves the right to close an account where a dispute cannot be resolved and continued activity would be inappropriate.

Where an account is closed due to a dispute without recovery, no fees or commission shall be payable by the Client in respect of that account, unless field visit fees or trace fees have already been incurred.

8. Field Visits

Field visits are conducted by Credvanta's agents or authorised field representatives for the purpose of making contact with the Debtor at their registered or known business or residential address.

Field visit agents are not enforcement agents, certificated bailiffs, or sheriff officers and have no powers of entry, seizure, or distress.

Field visit fees as set out in clause 3.2 are payable upon completion of each visit, regardless of whether contact with the Debtor is made or funds are recovered.

The Client acknowledges that field visits are conducted in a professional manner in compliance with applicable law and industry guidance and agrees not to instruct Credvanta to conduct any activity that would constitute harassment, intimidation, or any form of unlawful conduct.

9. Communications

Credvanta may contact Debtors using any or all of the following methods:

  • Postal correspondence
  • Telephone (Human and AI)
  • Email
  • SMS / text message
  • In person (field visits)

All communications with Debtors will be conducted in a professional and lawful manner and in accordance with applicable consumer and commercial communications regulations, including (where applicable) the FCA's guidelines on debt collection communications.

Credvanta may record telephone calls and communications for training, quality, compliance, and evidential purposes.

10. No Win, No Fee

Credvanta's standard recovery service operates on a no win, no fee basis, meaning that the fixed recovery fee and commission are only payable where Credvanta has successfully recovered funds from the Debtor.

The no win, no fee arrangement applies to the standard recovery service only. Field visit fees (clause 3.2) and trace fees (clause 3.3) are payable regardless of outcome where a visit has been conducted or a trace completed successfully.

"No fee" means no charge to the Client for Credvanta's standard recovery activities. It does not mean that the Client will not incur any costs whatsoever — any disbursements, third-party costs, or court fees instructed by the Client are payable by the Client directly.

11. Recovery of Costs & Interest

Where applicable under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 or any other applicable legislation, Credvanta may seek to recover statutory interest, compensation, and reasonable costs from the Debtor in addition to the principal debt.

Any statutory interest, compensation, or costs recovered from the Debtor shall form part of the recovered amount for the purposes of calculating Credvanta's commission.

Credvanta does not guarantee the recovery of any interest or costs and makes no representation as to the enforceability of any particular claim.

12. Payments

Where funds are received by Credvanta on behalf of the Client, Credvanta will account to the Client for such funds net of its commission and fees within a reasonable time following receipt.

Where a Debtor pays directly to the Client, the Client is responsible for notifying Credvanta and for remitting commission and any applicable fees to Credvanta within 14 days of receipt of cleared funds.

Credvanta does not hold client money and operates a zero-balance banking model in respect of recovered funds wherever possible. Where funds are held temporarily, they are held in a designated account and accounted for on a per-case basis.

Invoices issued by Credvanta for commission and fees are payable within 14 days of the invoice date. Late payment interest may be charged on overdue invoices at the statutory rate under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998.

13. Legal Escalation

Where Credvanta determines that pre-legal recovery activity has been unsuccessful, Credvanta may recommend escalation to legal proceedings.

Legal escalation is subject to the Client's separate written instruction and will be conducted through independent third-party solicitors or legal representatives. Credvanta is not itself a firm of solicitors and does not conduct litigation.

Any costs associated with legal proceedings, including court fees, solicitor fees, and enforcement costs, are the responsibility of the Client unless otherwise agreed in writing.
