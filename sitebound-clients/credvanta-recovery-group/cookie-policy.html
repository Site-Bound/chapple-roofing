Cookie Policy

How we use cookies on the Credvanta Recovery Group website

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to improve user experience, and to provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "session cookies" (which expire when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them). Cookies can be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third parties whose services are embedded in the website ("third-party cookies").

2. How We Use Cookies

The Credvanta Recovery Group website uses the following categories of cookies:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — These cookies are essential for the website to function correctly. They enable core functionality such as form submission and security. These cookies do not require your consent as they are strictly necessary for the operation of the website.
  • Analytics cookies — We use analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) to understand how visitors use our website, including which pages are visited most frequently, where visitors come from, and how long they spend on the site. This information helps us improve our website. Analytics cookies require your consent before being placed.
  • Functional cookies — These cookies remember your preferences and choices to provide a more personalised experience. Functional cookies require your consent.

Where required by law (including under the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations), we will obtain your consent before placing non-essential cookies on your device. You can manage your cookie preferences at any time via your browser settings or our cookie consent tool (where available).

3. Third-Party Cookies

Our website may include services provided by third parties that set their own cookies. These include:

  • Google Analytics — for website analytics and usage reporting. Google's privacy policy is available at policies.google.com/privacy.
  • Google Fonts — for font rendering. Google may set cookies or collect technical data in connection with font delivery.
  • Payment providers — where open banking or payment processing tools are embedded, the payment provider may set its own cookies. Please refer to the relevant provider's privacy and cookie policy for further information.

Credvanta does not control the cookies set by third-party services and is not responsible for the data practices of those third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy and cookie policies of any third-party services you interact with via our website.

4. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

  • Browser settings — Most web browsers allow you to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified when a cookie is set. Please refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions on how to manage cookies. Note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website.
  • Opt-out tools — For Google Analytics, you can opt out of tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, available at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.
  • Our cookie consent tool — Where we have implemented a cookie consent tool on our website, you can manage your preferences directly through that tool.

Please note that if you choose to refuse or delete cookies, some features of our website may not function as intended.

For further information about how we collect and use personal data, please refer to our full Data Protection & Privacy Policy.
