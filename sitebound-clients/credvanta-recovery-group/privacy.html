Data Protection & Privacy Policy

Applies to all clients, website users, employees, contractors, and business contacts

1. Introduction

Credvanta Recovery Group ("Credvanta", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of all individuals whose data we process. This policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect personal data in connection with our commercial debt recovery services, our website, and our internal operations.

This policy applies to all clients, website users, employees, contractors, and business contacts whose data we process. It should be read alongside our Debtor Privacy Notice and our Vulnerable Person / Business Policy.

2. Business Details

Credvanta Recovery Group is the trading name of Keiran Harvey, sole trader.

Contact: recover@credvanta.co.uk

Website: www.credvantarecoverygroup.com

Credvanta is not required to appoint a Data Protection Officer but takes its data protection obligations seriously and is committed to compliance with the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018.

3. Scope of This Policy

This policy covers all personal data processed by Credvanta in connection with:

  • The provision of commercial debt recovery services to clients;
  • Contact with debtors and individuals connected with commercial debt recovery activity;
  • The operation of our website;
  • Our employment and contractor relationships;
  • Our business development and marketing activities.

4. Types of Data We Process

Client Data

We process the following data in connection with our client relationships:

  • Business name, trading name, and registered address;
  • Names and contact details of individuals within the client business;
  • Banking and payment details;
  • Invoice and contractual documentation;
  • Communication records.

Debtor & Recovery Data

We process the following data in connection with debt recovery activity:

  • Names, addresses, and contact details of debtors and their representatives;
  • Business information, including company registration details;
  • Financial information, including amounts owed, payment history, and account status;
  • Invoice and contract documentation provided by the client;
  • Communication records, including letters, emails, call recordings, and SMS;
  • Credit reference and trace information;
  • Information relating to disputes, disputes of debt, or insolvency.

Employee & Contractor Data

We process the following data in respect of employees and contractors:

  • Name, address, and contact details;
  • National Insurance number and tax information;
  • Bank account details;
  • Employment history and qualifications;
  • Right to work documentation;
  • Performance and conduct records.

Website & Technical Data

We process the following data in connection with website use:

  • IP address and browser information;
  • Pages visited and time spent on the website;
  • Referral source;
  • Cookie data (see our Cookie Policy).

5. Special Category & Sensitive Data

Credvanta does not intentionally collect special category data (as defined in Article 9 UK GDPR) such as health, race, religion, or biometric data. However, in the course of debt recovery activity, individuals may voluntarily disclose sensitive information (for example, relating to financial hardship, health conditions, or personal circumstances). Where such information is shared with us, we will treat it with the utmost care and will only use it to ensure our approach to that individual is appropriate and sensitive to their circumstances.

6. How We Collect Data

We collect personal data in the following ways:

  • Directly from clients when they instruct us to recover a debt;
  • Directly from debtors and their representatives when they contact us;
  • From third-party trace agents, credit reference agencies, and public registers;
  • From Companies House and other publicly available sources;
  • Via our website (including forms, cookies, and analytics tools);
  • Via telephone, email, SMS, and other communications.

7. Purposes of Processing

We process personal data for the following purposes:

  • To provide commercial debt recovery services to our clients;
  • To contact debtors in connection with outstanding commercial debts;
  • To trace and locate debtors and their assets;
  • To negotiate and administer payment arrangements;
  • To maintain accurate records of recovery activity;
  • To manage our client relationships and billing;
  • To comply with our legal and regulatory obligations;
  • To prevent fraud and protect against unlawful activity;
  • To operate and improve our website;
  • To manage our employees and contractors;
  • To market our services to prospective clients (where lawful).

8. Legal Basis for Processing

We rely on the following legal bases for processing personal data:

  • Legitimate interests — the primary basis for processing debtor data in connection with commercial debt recovery activity. Credvanta and its clients have a legitimate interest in recovering lawfully owed commercial debts.
  • Contract — for processing client and employee/contractor data in connection with the performance of a contract.
  • Legal obligation — where processing is required to comply with applicable law.
  • Consent — where we rely on consent for specific activities (for example, certain marketing communications). Consent can be withdrawn at any time.

9. Controller & Processor Status

In relation to debtor personal data received from clients for the purposes of debt recovery, Credvanta acts as a data processor on behalf of the client (who is the data controller).

In relation to data collected directly by Credvanta (for example, from its website, in its own name, or for its own business purposes), Credvanta acts as the data controller.

A Data Processing Agreement is available for clients upon request.

10. Tracing, Screening & Compliance Checks

In the course of debt recovery activity, Credvanta may conduct or commission:

  • Trace and locate searches to find current contact details or addresses for debtors;
  • Credit reference agency checks to assess debtor information;
  • Insolvency and Companies House checks;
  • Anti-money laundering and fraud screening checks where appropriate.

These activities are carried out on the basis of Credvanta's legitimate interests and the legitimate interests of its clients in recovering lawfully owed commercial debts.

11. Communications Monitoring & Recording

Credvanta may record telephone calls and other communications with clients, debtors, and third parties for training, quality assurance, compliance, and evidential purposes. Where calls are recorded, individuals are informed at the start of the call. Recordings are retained for a limited period and are accessible only to authorised personnel.

12. Field Visits, CCTV & Body-Worn Cameras

Where field visits are conducted, Credvanta's field agents may use body-worn cameras for safety, evidence, and quality assurance purposes. Individuals are informed when a body-worn camera is in use. Footage is retained for a limited period and is accessible only to authorised personnel.

Where Credvanta's premises or operations involve CCTV, individuals are informed by appropriate signage and data is processed in accordance with this policy.

13. Automated Processing & Risk Scoring

Credvanta may use automated systems to assist with case management, prioritisation, and communication scheduling. Where automated processing is used, it does not constitute solely automated decision-making within the meaning of Article 22 UK GDPR — human review is applied to all material decisions affecting individuals.

14. Marketing & Advertising

Credvanta may use personal data to market its services to existing and prospective clients by email, telephone, or other means. B2B marketing communications to business contacts are carried out on the basis of legitimate interests. Where consent is required (for example, for direct marketing to individuals), we will obtain appropriate consent before sending marketing communications.

You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by contacting us at recover@credvanta.co.uk.

15. Website Tracking & Cookies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies. For full details, please refer to our Cookie Policy. We use analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) to understand how visitors use our website. Where required by law, we obtain consent before placing non-essential cookies.

16. Data Sharing

We may share personal data with the following categories of recipients:

  • Our clients (in relation to recovery activity conducted on their behalf);
  • Third-party trace agents and credit reference agencies;
  • Third-party legal representatives and solicitors (where legal escalation is instructed);
  • Field visit agents and representatives;
  • Technology and software providers (for case management, communications, and payment processing);
  • HM Revenue & Customs and other regulatory authorities (where required by law);
  • Courts and tribunals (where legal proceedings are commenced).

We do not sell personal data to third parties.

17. International Data Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, Credvanta ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR requirements, including standard contractual clauses or reliance on adequacy decisions. Where possible, Credvanta will use UK-based or adequacy-approved service providers.

18. Information Security

Credvanta takes appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or disclosure. These include:

  • Secure password management and access controls;
  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit and at rest where appropriate;
  • Regular review of access permissions;
  • Staff training on data protection and information security;
  • Secure disposal of data no longer required.

19. Cyber Security & Business Continuity

Credvanta maintains appropriate cyber security measures to protect its systems and data. In the event of a significant cyber incident or system failure, Credvanta has procedures in place to respond promptly, mitigate harm, and restore operations. Where a cyber incident affects personal data and meets the threshold for reporting, Credvanta will report to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) and notify affected individuals where required by law.

20. AI & Technology Usage

Credvanta may use artificial intelligence tools to assist with communications, case management, or analysis. Where AI is used in connection with personal data, Credvanta ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place and that AI tools are used in a manner consistent with this policy and applicable data protection law. AI-generated outputs are subject to human review and are not used as the sole basis for material decisions affecting individuals.

21. Data Retention

Credvanta retains personal data for as long as is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected and in accordance with applicable legal requirements. Our general retention periods are:

  • Client and recovery data: 6 years from the date of the last activity (to align with the Limitation Act 1980);
  • Call recordings: up to 12 months unless required for evidential purposes;
  • Employee and contractor data: 6 years from the end of employment/contract;
  • Website data: in accordance with our Cookie Policy.

Data may be retained for longer where required by law or where legitimate interests justify retention beyond the standard period.

22. Data Breaches & Incident Management

In the event of a personal data breach, Credvanta will assess the risk, take steps to contain and remediate the breach, and notify the ICO within 72 hours where the breach is likely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms. Affected individuals will be notified where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms.

23. Individual Rights

Individuals whose data we process have the following rights under UK GDPR:

  • Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you;
  • Right to rectification — to request correction of inaccurate data;
  • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your data in certain circumstances;
  • Right to restriction — to request that we limit how we use your data;
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format;
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing;
  • Rights relating to automated decision-making — not to be subject to solely automated decisions that significantly affect you.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at recover@credvanta.co.uk. We will respond within one calendar month. We may need to verify your identity before processing a request.

Please note that these rights are not absolute and may be limited in certain circumstances, including where processing is required for legal proceedings or to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

24. Children's Data

Credvanta does not knowingly collect or process the personal data of children under the age of 18. Our services are directed solely to businesses. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected data relating to a child, we will delete it promptly.

25. Complaints

If you have a concern about how we have handled your personal data, please contact us in the first instance at recover@credvanta.co.uk. If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Telephone: 0303 123 1113

26. Policy Updates

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. The current version will always be available on our website. We will notify clients and other affected parties of material changes where appropriate.

Debtor Privacy Notice

For individuals and business contacts connected with commercial debt recovery activity

1. Introduction

This Privacy Notice explains how Credvanta Recovery Group ("Credvanta", "we", "us") collects, uses, and shares your personal data in connection with commercial debt recovery activity. It applies to debtors, individuals connected with debtor businesses, and any other person whose data we process in connection with a commercial debt recovery matter.

2. Who We Are

Credvanta Recovery Group is the trading name of Keiran Harvey, sole trader, operating as a commercial debt recovery agent. We act on behalf of businesses (our clients) to recover outstanding commercial debts.

Contact: recover@credvanta.co.uk | 020 8129 1490

3. Types of Data We May Collect

Identification & Contact Information

  • Full name and title
  • Business name and trading name
  • Registered and trading addresses
  • Telephone numbers (landline and mobile)
  • Email addresses

Financial & Account Information

  • Amount and details of the outstanding debt
  • Invoice and contract documentation
  • Payment history
  • Bank account details (where provided for payment purposes)
  • Information about assets or ability to pay

Business & Corporate Information

  • Company registration number and status
  • Directors, partners, and sole traders associated with the business
  • Companies House filings and public register information
  • Insolvency status

Communication & Behavioural Data

  • Records of correspondence (letters, emails, SMS)
  • Call recordings
  • Notes of conversations and visits
  • Responses to communications

4. How We Collect Data

We collect your data from the following sources:

  • From our client (the creditor) — who provides us with your details as part of the debt recovery instruction;
  • Directly from you — when you contact us by telephone, email, letter, or online form;
  • From third-party trace agents and credit reference agencies;
  • From publicly available sources including Companies House, the Insolvency Register, and electoral roll data;
  • From field visit agents conducting visits to your known address.

5. How We Use Data

We use your data to:

  • Contact you in connection with an outstanding commercial debt;
  • Verify your identity and business details;
  • Assess the status and enforceability of the debt;
  • Negotiate and administer payment arrangements;
  • Maintain accurate records of all recovery activity;
  • Escalate matters to legal representatives if required;
  • Comply with our legal and regulatory obligations.

6. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your data on the basis of legitimate interests — specifically, the legitimate interest of our client in recovering a lawfully owed commercial debt, and Credvanta's legitimate interest in providing debt recovery services on behalf of its clients.

We have conducted a legitimate interests assessment and are satisfied that our processing does not override your fundamental rights and freedoms, having regard to the commercial nature of the debt and the professional manner in which recovery activity is conducted.

7. Communications & Call Recording

We may contact you by post, telephone, email, and SMS in connection with the outstanding debt. Telephone calls may be recorded for training, quality, compliance, and evidential purposes. Where a call is recorded, you will be informed at the start of the call.

8. Field Visits & Monitoring

Where a field visit is conducted, a Credvanta representative or authorised agent may attend your business or known address. Field agents may use body-worn cameras for safety and evidence purposes and will inform you if a camera is in use. Field agents are not enforcement agents and have no powers of entry, seizure, or distress.

9. Tracing & Locate Activity

Where current contact details or address information is not available, Credvanta may conduct or commission a trace search using third-party trace agents and credit reference agencies. Trace searches use publicly available and commercially licensed data to locate current contact information. Individuals are not directly notified of a trace search in advance, as advance notification would defeat the purpose of the search.

10. Data Sharing

We may share your data with:

  • Our client (the creditor) — in connection with the recovery of the debt;
  • Third-party legal representatives — where the matter is escalated to legal proceedings;
  • Credit reference agencies and trace agents;
  • Technology providers (case management, communications, payment processing);
  • Courts and tribunals (where legal proceedings are commenced);
  • Regulatory authorities (where required by law).

We do not sell your personal data to third parties.

11. Payments & Open Banking

Where you make a payment in connection with an outstanding debt, payment may be processed via our open banking payment provider or another payment processing platform. Payment data is processed securely and is used solely for the purposes of facilitating and recording payment. Credvanta does not store full card details.

12. International Data Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR requirements. Credvanta will use UK-based or adequacy-approved service providers wherever possible.

13. Website Tracking & Cookies

If you visit our website, we may collect technical data including your IP address, browser type, and pages visited. For full details, please refer to our Cookie Policy.

14. Data Retention

We retain personal data relating to debt recovery activity for 6 years from the date of the last activity on the account, in line with the Limitation Act 1980. Call recordings are generally retained for up to 12 months unless required for evidential purposes. Data may be retained for longer where legal proceedings are ongoing or where a legitimate interest justifies extended retention.

15. Data Security

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, or disclosure. Access to personal data is restricted to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis.

16. Individual Rights

You have the following rights under UK GDPR:

  • Right of access — to request a copy of your personal data;
  • Right to rectification — to request correction of inaccurate data;
  • Right to erasure — in certain circumstances;
  • Right to restriction — to limit how we use your data;
  • Right to object — to processing based on legitimate interests.

Please note that the right to erasure and the right to object may be limited where processing is necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims or to comply with a legal obligation. To exercise your rights, contact us at recover@credvanta.co.uk.

17. Complaints

If you have a concern about how we have handled your personal data, please contact us at recover@credvanta.co.uk. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or 0303 123 1113.

18. Policy Updates

We may update this Notice from time to time. The current version is always available on our website.

Vulnerable Person / Business Policy

Our approach to supporting individuals in sensitive or vulnerable circumstances

1. Purpose

Credvanta Recovery Group is committed to treating all individuals it contacts in a fair, respectful, and appropriate manner. This policy sets out how Credvanta identifies and responds to situations where an individual or business may be in vulnerable or sensitive circumstances.

2. Identifying Vulnerability

Vulnerability may arise in many forms and may be temporary, cyclical, or long-term. Indicators of potential vulnerability that Credvanta's staff are trained to recognise include:

  • Expressed financial distress or inability to pay;
  • References to mental health difficulties, illness, or bereavement;
  • Confusion, distress, or unusual communication patterns during contact;
  • Indications of domestic abuse or personal crisis;
  • Reference to insolvency, administration, or business failure;
  • Requests for additional time or support.

3. Approach

Where Credvanta identifies or suspects vulnerability, we will:

  • Treat the individual with sensitivity and respect at all times;
  • Adapt our communications approach to reflect the individual's circumstances;
  • Pause or adjust recovery activity where appropriate;
  • Signpost the individual to relevant support organisations (such as StepChange, National Debtline, Citizens Advice, or Business Debtline);
  • Note the circumstances on the account to ensure consistent treatment;
  • Notify the client where the circumstances may affect the recovery approach;
  • Not use aggressive, intimidating, or oppressive tactics at any time.

4. Limitations

Credvanta undertakes commercial business-to-business (B2B) debt recovery activity only and is not a consumer debt collection service. However, Credvanta acknowledges that individuals connected with businesses — including sole traders, partners, directors, and personal guarantors — may face personal financial difficulty and that recovery activity can have a real impact on individuals as well as businesses. Credvanta's commitment to fair and sensitive treatment applies in all cases, regardless of the commercial nature of the underlying debt.

Credvanta Recovery Group undertakes commercial business-to-business (B2B) debt recovery activity only. Credvanta does not undertake regulated consumer debt collection activity and is not authorised or regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). Credvanta does not provide legal advice, insolvency advice, debt counselling, debt adjusting, or regulated financial services or other regulated activities.
