Debtor Payment Plan Terms

Terms that apply when entering into a payment arrangement via Credvanta Recovery Group

1. Payment Arrangements

Any payment arrangement entered into via Credvanta Recovery Group ("Credvanta") constitutes a legally binding agreement between the Debtor and the Creditor (the business to whom the debt is owed). Credvanta acts solely as agent for the Creditor in administering the arrangement.

Payment arrangements are subject to approval by the Creditor and may be varied or cancelled where the Debtor fails to comply with the agreed terms.

2. Payment Methods

Payments under a payment arrangement must be made by one of the following methods:

  • Standing order or bank transfer to the Creditor's nominated account;
  • Debit card or credit card payment via our secure payment portal;
  • Open banking payment via our open banking provider.

Cash payments are not accepted. Cheque payments are only accepted where specifically agreed in writing.

3. Continuous Payment Authority

Where a Debtor authorises payment by debit or credit card on a recurring basis, this constitutes a Continuous Payment Authority (CPA). Under a CPA:

  • The Debtor authorises Credvanta or its payment provider to collect payments on the agreed dates and in the agreed amounts;
  • The Debtor may cancel a CPA at any time by notifying Credvanta in writing or by contacting their card issuer;
  • Cancellation of a CPA does not cancel the underlying debt or payment arrangement — alternative payment must be arranged immediately upon cancellation.

4. Default

A Debtor will be in default of a payment arrangement where:

  • A scheduled payment is missed or returned unpaid;
  • A CPA is cancelled without an alternative payment method being agreed;
  • The Debtor fails to maintain agreed payments for two or more consecutive periods.

In the event of default, the Creditor (through Credvanta) reserves the right to cancel the payment arrangement, demand immediate payment of the full outstanding balance, and take further recovery or legal action.

5. No Waiver

The acceptance of a payment arrangement does not constitute a waiver of the Creditor's right to recover the full outstanding balance, to charge statutory interest or late payment compensation, or to pursue legal proceedings in the event of default. A payment arrangement is an accommodation offered to the Debtor and does not affect the Creditor's legal rights.

Creditor Relationship, Payment Arrangements & Regulatory Status

Information about Credvanta's role and regulatory position

Any payment arrangement entered into is made directly between the Debtor and the Creditor.

Credvanta acts solely as a commercial debt recovery agent on behalf of the Creditor and is authorised to communicate, negotiate, and administer payment arrangements in accordance with the Creditor's instructions.

Credvanta is not a party to the underlying contract, invoice, or debt agreement between the Debtor and the Creditor.

Once a payment arrangement has been agreed, a separate repayment plan document confirming the agreed terms, payment schedule, and arrangement details may be issued for confirmation and record purposes.

All payments are made directly to the Creditor using the payment details provided or via our open banking payment provider. Credvanta does not hold, receive, process, or control client money.

Where payment links, open banking technology, or automated payment systems are used, such systems are provided solely to facilitate direct payment to the Creditor and to allow payment status notifications relating to the arrangement.

Credvanta undertakes unregulated commercial business-to-business (B2B) debt recovery activity only and is not authorised or regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA).

Credvanta does not provide legal advice, insolvency advice, regulated debt advice, debt counselling, debt adjusting, or regulated financial services. Any payment arrangement discussed or agreed is based solely on information provided by the Debtor and the Creditor's instructions.

If you are struggling with debt, free and independent support is available from StepChange (stepchange.org), National Debtline (nationaldebtline.org), Citizens Advice (citizensadvice.org.uk), and Business Debtline (businessdebtline.org).
