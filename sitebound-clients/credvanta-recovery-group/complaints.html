Complaints Procedure

How Credvanta handles complaints from clients, debtors, and third parties

1. Purpose

Credvanta Recovery Group ("Credvanta") is committed to providing a professional and fair service. We take all complaints seriously and aim to resolve them promptly and fairly. This procedure sets out how complaints are received, investigated, and resolved.

To submit a complaint, email: Complaints@Credvanta.co.uk

2. Who Can Make a Complaint

A complaint may be made by:

  • Any client instructing Credvanta;
  • Any debtor or individual contacted by Credvanta in connection with debt recovery activity;
  • Any third party affected by Credvanta's actions.

A complaint may be made by the individual themselves or by an authorised representative acting on their behalf (such as a solicitor, debt advice charity, or other authorised agent).

3. How to Make a Complaint

Complaints should be submitted in writing wherever possible. You can submit a complaint by:

  • Email: Complaints@Credvanta.co.uk
  • Post: Credvanta Recovery Group, marked for the attention of the Complaints Handler

When submitting a complaint, please include:

  • Your full name and contact details;
  • A reference number or account number (if known);
  • A clear description of your complaint and the outcome you are seeking;
  • Copies of any relevant documentation or correspondence.

4. Types of Complaints Covered

This procedure covers complaints relating to:

  • The conduct of Credvanta's staff or representatives;
  • The manner or frequency of communications (including letters, calls, emails, or SMS);
  • Field visits conducted on Credvanta's behalf;
  • Inaccurate information or errors in correspondence;
  • Handling of personal data;
  • Failure to respond to disputes or queries;
  • Billing or commission disputes (for clients);
  • Any other aspect of Credvanta's service.

5. Acknowledgement of Complaints

Credvanta will acknowledge receipt of a complaint within 5 business days of receiving it. The acknowledgement will confirm that the complaint has been received and provide a reference number and the name of the person handling the complaint.

6. Investigation Process

Upon receipt of a complaint, Credvanta will:

  • Assign the complaint to an appropriate member of staff for investigation;
  • Review all relevant records, correspondence, and documentation relating to the complaint;
  • Contact the complainant if additional information is required;
  • Where relevant, review call recordings, written communications, and case notes;
  • Provide a full written response setting out the findings of the investigation and the outcome.

Credvanta aims to provide a full response within 20 business days of receipt of the complaint. Where an investigation requires more time, Credvanta will notify the complainant of the reason for the delay and provide an expected resolution date.

7. Recovery Activity During a Complaint

Where a complaint is received from a debtor or third party, Credvanta will review the complaint on its merits and may, at its discretion, pause or adjust recovery activity whilst the complaint is investigated. Credvanta is not obliged to suspend all activity pending investigation but will act proportionately and sensitively in all cases.

Where a debtor raises a complaint that also constitutes a dispute of the underlying debt, the complaint will be handled under this procedure and the client will be notified of the dispute.

8. Vulnerable Persons & Sensitive Circumstances

Where a complaint indicates that the complainant may be in vulnerable or sensitive circumstances (including financial distress, mental health difficulties, or bereavement), Credvanta will handle the complaint with additional sensitivity and care in accordance with its Vulnerable Person / Business Policy.

If you require additional support in making or pursuing a complaint, please let us know and we will do what we can to accommodate your needs.

9. Outcomes

Following investigation, the outcome of a complaint may include one or more of the following:

  • An apology where Credvanta has made an error or acted incorrectly;
  • A correction of inaccurate information;
  • A change in the approach to communications or recovery activity;
  • A refund or adjustment of fees (where applicable);
  • Closure of an account (where appropriate);
  • An explanation where Credvanta does not uphold the complaint.

10. Appeals Process

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of your complaint, you may request a review by writing to Credvanta within 20 business days of receiving the outcome letter. Your request should set out clearly why you believe the outcome was incorrect and provide any additional information you wish us to consider.

A review will be conducted by a senior member of staff who was not involved in the original investigation. A final response will be provided within 20 business days of the review request being received.

11. External Options

Credvanta is not currently a member of an external alternative dispute resolution (ADR) scheme. If you are not satisfied with the outcome of your complaint following review, external options may include:

  • The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) — for complaints relating to data protection (ico.org.uk / 0303 123 1113);
  • Citizens Advice — for guidance on your rights (citizensadvice.org.uk);
  • Legal action through the courts — as a last resort.

12. Unreasonable or Abusive Complaints

Credvanta is committed to handling all complaints fairly and professionally. However, Credvanta reserves the right to decline to continue investigating or responding to a complaint where the complainant behaves in an abusive, threatening, or unreasonably persistent manner towards Credvanta's staff. In such cases, Credvanta will write to the complainant explaining its decision and directing them to external options where appropriate.

13. Complaint Records

Credvanta maintains records of all complaints received, including the nature of the complaint, the investigation, the outcome, and any corrective actions taken. These records are used to identify patterns, improve our services, and demonstrate compliance with our complaints obligations. Complaint records are retained for 6 years from the date of closure.
